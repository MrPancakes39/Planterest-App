Months

January

  • The stillness and the winter rest stage.
  • Winter trees rest stage.
  • Pruning of some trees that bear the frost, such as apples and cherries.
  • It is recommended to sterilize pruning equipment after using it with trees affected by diseases with solution of tar.

February

  • Winter rest stage.
  • Continuous pruning of fruit trees.
  • Sprinkle mineral oil with the butcher in the mid-height areas.
  • Sowing summer vegetable nurseries, such as tomatoes - capsicum - eggplant...
  • Planting onions (for mortar), chickpeas, and some green leafy vegetables.

March

  • The stage of entry into force of the vegetable sap.
  • Complete the cultivation of onions and leafy vegetables.
  • Complete the pruning of fruit trees.
  • Monitor the appearance of some diseases, such as septoria.
  • Preparing the land for planting some summer vegetables.
  • Pen vaccination stage.
  • Cut the herbs before flowering before the seeds are formed.
  • Bloom of some fruitful trees.

April

  • The stage of blossoming buds.
  • Tilling the land.
  • Monitor the emergence and control of pests.
  • Preparing the land for planting summer vegetables.
  • Control pests, if any, within the integrated pest management (IPM).
  • The beginning of holding fruits and the emergence of some of it.
  • Start planting some summer vegetables.
  • Apply fermented compost before planting vegetables.
  • Almond picking.

May

  • The stage of the appearance of fruits.
  • Putting pheromones and food traps in orchards to monitor the appearance of the first generation of Drosophila.
  • Monitor the pests and control it.
  • Complete the tillage and cultivation process.
  • Irrigation of fruitful trees begins when the rain is cut off early.
  • Start soluble chemical fertilization with irrigation.
  • Weeding leafy vegetables.
  • Greengage picking.
  • Ripening of cherry fruits early.
  • Spray calcium fertilizer on apple orchards in areas where it rains and accumulates snow more than 25 cm

June

  • Follow up of field works such as irrigation, weeding, control and fertilization...
  • Planting autumn vegetables like cabbage, cauliflower, broccoli...
  • Grafting trees with eyes or a patch.
  • Summer pruning, i.e. removing branches and growth in bad places.
  • The trees begin to naturally spread their fruits, and the manual unloading begins with the trees that hold a lot.
  • Follow picking peaches, prunus, and apricots.

July

  • Follow- up of fields works such as irrigation, weeding control, fertilization and summer pruning, especially for vine.
  • Complete picking peaches, prunus, and apricots.
  • Pick some types of vegetables.
  • Pick the early apple varieties.

August

  • Follow-up of fields works such as irrigation, weeding control, fertilization...
  • Observe the ripening period of some apple varieties.
  • Stop the fertilization.
  • Control three weeks before picking.

September

  • Follow-up the necessary fields works such as irrigation for late cultivators and weeding, stopping control and fertilization.
  • Pick the nut when it starts peeling.
  • Observe the ripening of apples that bear the cooling.
  • Start picking apples.
  • Cultivating some autumn varieties such as radish, spinach, cress and potatoes.
  • Start planting vegetables in greenhouses.

October

  • Harvest some late-apple varieties, such as the granite smith and fudgy.
  • Begin harvesting the olives two weeks after to convert the largest possible quantity of oil, especially for rain fed trees.
  • Starting to prepare the ground for planting some varieties of vegetable such as garlic, potatoes, beans, and winter onions.
  • Cultivation of wheat and barley and some crops such and beams and others.

November

  • The pruning of fruits trees begins after 80% of its leaves have fallen off.
  • Orchards and fields planted with fruits trees, i.e. spraying area 46% at a rate of 12 kg per 100 liters of water.
  • Deep tillage after spraying area directly to bury the leaves.
  • Collet the infected fruits that are stuck on the tress, burry them deep in the soil or give them to some animals as feed.
  • Pruning the infected branches with ulcers, collecting all waste and burning it in the orchard.
  • Place the fermented compost in trenches dug around the trunks of fruitful trees with a depth of about 20 cm and a width of 30 cm, provided that these trenches are parallel to the branches of the tree.
  • Begin planting the monument of fruitful tress. It is preferable that this monument has its leaves fallen, and in the event that it is necessary to plant it before its leaves fall, it is preferable to drop the leaves manually.

December

  • Follow-up cultivation of some varieties of vegetables and field crops.
  • Follow the process of pruning fruitful trees.
  • Follow up the planting of fruitful trees.
  • Put the compound chemical fertilizer such aa 15-15-15 in the same way you apply an organic fertilizer.
